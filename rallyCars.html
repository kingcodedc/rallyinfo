Rally cars

Rally cars are versatile and designed for various terrain conditions and types of competitions. One of the most well-known is the WRC (World Rally Championship) class car, which is designed to compete in diverse terrains around the world. These cars are typically four-wheel drive with powerful engines and advanced suspension systems. Another example would be rallycross cars, designed for speed and agility, emphasizing acceleration and maneuverability on short track segments. Additionally, there are historical rally cars resembling old racing models, often used in demonstration runs and special competitions!

WRC (World Rally Championship) used in the WRC series, the highest level of rally racing. Typically four-wheel-drive and powerful cars, such as Toyota Gazoo Racing Yaris WRC, Hyundai i20 Coupe WRC, and Ford Puma WRC. Powerful engines, lightweight chassis, and advanced aerodynamics. Performance-enhancing technologies like active differentials and adjustable suspension systems.

Group R5 used in the WRC2 series and many national rally championships. Four-wheel-drive cars based on production models, such as Ford Fiesta R5, Škoda Fabia R5, and Citroën C3 R5. Powerful but more economical engines compared to WRC cars. Limited aerodynamics and electronics but still competitive performance.

Group B historical rally group that was used in the 1980s. Known for extreme performance and technical advancements. Legendary cars like Audi Quattro, Lancia Delta S4, and Ford RS200. High power-to-weight ratio making them extremely dangerous, leading to the group being banned for safety reasons.

Group N rally group based on production cars. Used in several national and regional rally championships. Limited technology compared to WRC and R5 cars but still competitive and more budget-friendly. Examples include Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution and Subaru Impreza WRX STI.

Rally Super 2000 (S2000) former rally group used in the WRC2 class before the introduction of R5 cars. Four-wheel-drive cars based on production models offering competitive performance. Examples include Škoda Fabia S2000, Peugeot 207 S2000, and Ford Fiesta S2000.

Rally Kit Car rally group popular in the 1990s. Based on smaller and lighter cars with more powerful engines but limited aerodynamics and technology. Examples include Citroën Saxo Kit Car, Peugeot 306 Maxi, and Renault Clio Kit Car.

Historic Rally Cars rally cars originating from historical periods like the 1960s and 1970s. Often rear-wheel-drive and less powerful than modern rally cars. Famous models include Ford Escort MKII, Lancia Stratos, and Porsche 911.
